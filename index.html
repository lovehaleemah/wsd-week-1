Who is Ina May?

Ina May Gaskin is a pioneer in her field, she has often been called the founder of authentic midwifery. In 1971 her and her husband Stephen founded a commune and called it the farm. At the farm the women formed the first out of hospital birth center. From there she became famous for her apparent talent for being a midwife and doula. Women did and still do travel a long way just to give birth on the farm, with the hope of Ina's attendance. Her loving and calm presence has been a life saver for hundreds of women who long to opt out of the harsh environment of a hospital birth. In the homebirth community, her books are standard reads. Ina May is also an accomplished author and speaker. She recently appeared in the famous movie "The Business of Being Born" by Rikki Lake. And to much of her follower's pleasure, a biographical movie has just been released this year. "Birth Story: Ina May Gaskin & The Farm Midwives". Which chronicles her life and experiences at the farm. She continues to be an inspiration to midwives, doulas, and all who are passionate about natural childbirth, bringing awareness and knowledge to the general community about the benefits of non-medicalized, out-of- hospital birth.
